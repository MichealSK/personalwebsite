Programming

Programming is one of my big passions, which is why I chose to enroll into
an Information Technology University in the first place.
Knowing me as someone who has a deep appreciation for the arts, people were
initially confused as to why I did not instead take part of an arts university
instead.

The answer? I think programming is an art in of itself, with more subtlety and
need for finer attention to detail and technical knowledge compared to any other
if you want it to be as optimal as possible.
Out of every other art, programming is what I want to work on professionally.



Visual Design, Writing and Music

I appreciate all forms of art: movie, sculpture, painting and drawing. I find
enjoyment in observing them and find fulfillment in spending my time indulging
in them and oftentimes analysing and giving constructive criticism to.

The ones that I enjoy most though are the ones I am finding enjoyment in creating
myself: Visual Design, Writing and Music.
I am currently writing my own novel where a young detective is trying to solve a
series of supernatural murders and I sketch out my own concept character and
technology artwork for it.

Using the knowledge I gained from my musical education as well as my own personal
research, I compose my own music: mostly neoclassical and techno pieces.



Videogame Design

There is one more art that I think has not been getting the appreciation it deserves,
or even the title I think it deserves: an art-form!
The only art-form that gets close to rivaling programming for me is videogame design.

I have been using my programming knowledge to make mods for videogames for quite a while,
in fact, it is how I got into programming in the first place.
I have made videogame mods that have recieved recognition in their various communities
and have made a few amateur standalone videogames. I fully plan on continuing my passion
for videogame design in the future, especially when my programming knowledge deepens.
